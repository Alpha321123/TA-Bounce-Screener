IPO tracker
Recent and upcoming IPOs on ASX and US markets.
What is an IPO? Initial Public Offering — a private company sells shares to the public for the first time. Early investors can profit if the stock rises. But: many IPOs drop below listing price within months. Risks include hype-driven pricing and lock-up expiry (when insiders can sell). Strategy: wait 1–3 months post-listing before buying to see how the stock settles. Read the prospectus. For ASX IPOs: ASX.com.au. For US: StockAnalysis.com.

Auto-simulation
Simulates buying all stocks with bounce score >80 and selling when sell signal triggers. Tests strategy efficiency.
How it works: Every time you run a scan, the simulation checks if any stock had score >80 on that day. It "buys" at the current price and "sells" when RSI exceeds 65 or price is 15%+ above SMA20. All history is recorded. This tells you whether the bounce score strategy would have been profitable. Note: Past simulation results do not guarantee future returns.
