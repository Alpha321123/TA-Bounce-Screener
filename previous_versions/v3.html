IPO tracker
Recent and upcoming IPOs. IPOs can be profitable but carry high risk — read the explanation below before investing.
What is an IPO? An Initial Public Offering is when a private company sells shares to the public for the first time. Early investors can profit if the stock rises after listing — but IPOs are also very risky: many companies drop below their listing price within months. Key risks: no trading history, hype-driven pricing, lock-up period expiry (when early investors can sell, often crashing the price). Strategy: wait 1–3 months after listing to see how the stock settles before buying.
